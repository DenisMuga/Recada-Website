About Recada

Resource Institute for Community and Human Development Agency (RECADA) is a local NGO registered by the government of Kenya in 2003. The main purpose of RECADA is to empower and facilitate community and holistic human development. RECADA collaborates with churches, other NGOs, institution of learning and individuals in promoting and supporting socio-cultural, economic and human development in institutions, communities and vulnerable groups; through a multi-sectoral and integrated approach for sustainable livelihood. The NGO is currently running several projects in different parts of Kenya both in rural and urban areas; including, primary schools, entrepreneurship programs, skills trainings, general agriculture and horticultural faming and water project for the elderly. Recada Projects and Programs

Recada Educational Center

Grace has been passionate about children ministry. For 18 years Grace worked with Compassion International Kenya an organization working with children in many parts of Kenya. Grace resigned later in 2012 and has since started a school in Mathare area 4B. The project is targeting Highly Vulnerable Children (HVC) who are not attending school (due to various reasons) within Mathare slums and Sondu rural.

BFED Program

Believers Fellowship Entrepreneurship Development (BFED) project was started in 2007, with the first group consisting of five church members (four ladies and a young man) from our local church, OCC-Believers Fellowship Tabernacle (Lucky Summer). This number has since grown to fifty members. After the experience gained from working with the Lucky-summer as a pilot group and local church members who are staying in Mathare slum as a special group; Rev. Gabriel and Grace have since moved on to launch the Bondo/Rarieda agricultural and horticultural group. The new group is modeled in a rural set up.The direct beneficiaries of this project are needy community members both men and women. The emphasis is on the younger generations who are more vulnerable, and easily get involved in diverse negative activities when not engaged.

NIFED Program

As an organization, we have carried out a preliminary survey and realized the need to extend the entrepreneurship program to the rural areas.
